# Biryani Recipe
## Ingredients
### For the Rice:
- 2 cups Basmati rice
- 4 cups water
- 2-3 cloves
- 2-3 cardamom pods
- 1 small cinnamon stick
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 tsp salt
### For the Meat/Vegetables:
- 500g chicken/mutton/vegetables (cut into medium-sized pieces)
- 100g stockfish
- 1 cup plain yogurt
- 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
- 1 tsp turmeric powder
- 1 tsp red chili powder
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp coriander powder
- Salt to taste
### For the Biryani Base:
- 3 tbsp ghee or oil
- 2 large onions (thinly sliced)
- 2 tomatoes (chopped)
- 2 green chilies (slit lengthwise)
- 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
- 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves (chopped)
- 1/4 cup milk
- A pinch of saffron (optional)
- 1 tsp kewra or rose water (optional)
### Optional Garnish:
- Fried onions
- Cashews and raisins (fried in ghee)
---
## Instructions
### Step 0: Prepare the Rice
1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
2. In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, bay leaf, and salt.
3. Add the soaked rice and cook until it is 70% done (grains should still be firm). Drain and set aside.
---
### Step 2: Marinate the Meat/Vegetables
1. In a bowl, mix the meat/vegetables with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, coriander powder, and salt.
2. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or up to 4 hours for better flavor).
---
### Step 3: Cook the Biryani Base
1. Heat ghee or oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot.
2. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
3. Add tomatoes and green chilies, and cook until the tomatoes are soft.
4. Add the marinated meat/vegetables and cook until they are tender and the oil separates from the mixture.
5. Stir in half of the chopped coriander and mint leaves.
---
### Step 4: Layer the Biryani
1. In the same pot, spread half of the cooked rice over the meat/vegetable mixture.
2. Sprinkle the remaining coriander and mint leaves on top.
3. Add the remaining rice as the final layer.
4. Warm the milk and dissolve the saffron in it. Drizzle this mixture over the rice. Add kewra or rose water if using.
5. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. If the lid is not tight, seal it with dough to trap the steam.
---
### Step 5: Dum Cooking
1. Place the pot on low heat and cook for 20-25 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld together.
2. Alternatively, you can place the pot on a tawa (flat griddle) to prevent direct heat and cook for the same duration.
---
### Step 6: Serve
1. Gently mix the layers before serving to ensure even distribution of flavors.
2. Garnish with fried onions, cashews, and raisins if desired.
3. Serve hot with raita, salad, or a side of your choice.
---
Enjoy your delicious homemade biryani! 🍛
