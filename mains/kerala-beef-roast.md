# Kerala Beef Roast

Kerala beef roast, also known as beef ularthiyathu, is a spicy dry-fried dish with black pepper, curry leaves, coconut oil, and slow-cooked onions. It is best served with flaky kerala porotta.

---

## Ingredients

- 500 g beef, cut into small cubes
- 2 tbsp coconut oil
- 2 large onions, thinly sliced
- 1 tomato, chopped
- 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
- 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
- 2 sprigs curry leaves
- 1/2 cup sliced coconut pieces, optional
- 1 tsp turmeric powder
- 2 tsp coriander powder
- 1 1/2 tsp red chili powder
- Lots of freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1/2 tsp fennel powder
- 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
- Salt, to taste
- 1/2 cup water

---

## Instructions

1. **Marinate the beef**  
   Mix the beef with turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, black pepper, garam masala, fennel powder, ginger-garlic paste, salt, and a few curry leaves. Rest for at least 30 minutes.

2. **Cook until tender**  
   Add the marinated beef and water to a heavy pot or pressure cooker. Cook until the beef is tender and most of the liquid has reduced.

3. **Build the roast base**  
   Heat coconut oil in a wide pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them crackle. Add onions, green chilies, coconut pieces, and curry leaves, then saute until the onions turn deep golden brown.

4. **Roast the beef**  
   Add the cooked beef and tomato to the pan. Stir well and cook over medium-low heat until the masala clings to the beef and the edges begin to crisp.

5. **Finish and serve**  
   Taste and adjust salt or pepper. Finish with extra curry leaves and serve hot.

---

## Tips

- For a darker roast, cook slowly in a wide pan and stir often.
- Add more black pepper at the end for a sharper Kerala-style heat.
- Coconut oil and curry leaves are important for the classic flavor.
